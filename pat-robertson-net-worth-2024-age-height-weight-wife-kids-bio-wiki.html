Early Life

Marion Gordon “Pat” Robertson was born in Lexington, Virginia, on March 22, 1930, to his parents Gladys Churchill and Absalom Willis Robertson. He was nicknamed “Pat” by his older brother Willis.

He completed his schooling at Chattanooga’s The McCallie School in 1946. Following that, he received his bachelor’s degree in History from Washington and Lee University. In there, he was a member of the Sigma Alpha Epsilon fraternity.

Personal Life

In August 1954 Pat Robertson got married to Adelia “Dede” Elmer. The couple has got four children named Elizabeth Faith Robertson, Anne Carter Robertson, Tim Robertson, and Gordon P. Robertson. They also have fourteen grandchildren and seven great-grandchildren.

He is an activist and has been involved in political service. Robertson has also been involved in controversies and has been criticised for his comments.

Age, Height, and Weight

Being born on 22 March 1930, Pat Robertson is 94 years old as of today’s date 20th July 2024. His height is 1.73 m tall, and weight is 79 kg.

Career

Pat Robertson began his career by getting drafted in the military. He was reinstated in 1948. In 1952, he was promoted to a first lieutenant. In 1955, he obtained his degree in law from Yale Law School. In 1959, he earned his degree in Master of Divinity from New York’s The Biblical Seminary.

In 1961, Robertson was ordained as a minister in the Southern Baptist Convention. A year later, he started the Christian Broadcasting Network in Virginia. He began it by purchasing a station in Portsmouth. In 1977, after buying the channel, he renamed it as CBN.

The network CBN is currently available in 180 countries and has been broadcasted in 71 languages. In 1988, it was renamed as CBN Family Channel and later just as Family Channel. Two years later, he founded International Family Entertainment Inc.

He had also founded the CBN University in Virginia Beach in 1977. In 1989, it was renamed as Regent University. He became the founder and served as the president of the American Center for Law & Justice.

Awards & Achievements

In 2017, Pat Robertson was honoured at the Movieguide Awards. In 1984, the Women’s National Republican Club named him as the Man of the Year Award. He was also named as the Man of the Year in 1988 by the Students of America. In 2000, he won the award of Cross of Nails. He has also received the Faith and Freedom Award. In 1994, the Christians’ Israel Public Action Campaign conferred him with the Defender of Israel Award.
