FAQs about Louis Lortie

Who is Louis Lortie?

He is a renowned French-Canadian pianist known for his interpretations of a broad spectrum of the keyboard canon. He is praised for his fresh perspective and unique contributions to piano music.

What are some of Lortie’s major achievements?

He won the first prize at the Ferruccio Busoni International Piano Competition in 1984. He has also been a prizewinner at the Leeds Competition and was appointed an Officer of the Order of Canada and a Knight of the National Order of Quebec.

Which major orchestras have he played with?

Lortie has performed with many prestigious orchestras worldwide, including the BBC Philharmonic, Cleveland Orchestra, Orchestre National de France, and the Philadelphia Orchestra.

What is unique about Lortie’s music style?

He is known for his comprehensive and interpretative approach to classical piano, particularly noted for complete cycle performances such as all Chopin Etudes in one evening, Beethoven’s 32 sonatas, and the complete works of Ravel.

Where has he performed?

He has appeared in major concert halls like Carnegie Hall, the Concertgebouw in Amsterdam, and the Philharmonie de Paris.

Does he have any roles in music education or festivals?

Yes, he was a master in residence at the Queen Elisabeth Music Chapel in Brussels and is the co-founder and artistic director of the LacMus Festival on Lake Como.

What recordings has he made?

His discography includes over 45 recordings for Chandos Records, covering major works by Chopin, Beethoven, Ravel, and Liszt, among others.

Has he received any honorary titles or awards?

Besides his competition prizes, Lortie has been honored as an Officer of the Order of Canada, a Knight of the National Order of Quebec, and has received an honorary doctorate from Université Laval.

What recent projects has he been involved in?

Lortie has recently focused on performing and recording contemporary works, including those by British composer Thomas Adès and has engaged in extensive Beethoven and Mozart projects.

What educational background does he have?

He studied in Montreal with Yvonne Hubert, in Vienna with Dieter Weber, and also with Leon Fleisher.
