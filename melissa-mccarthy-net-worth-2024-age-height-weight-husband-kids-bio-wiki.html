Early Life

Melissa McCarthy was born on August 26, 1970, in Plainfield, Illinois, Chicago. Her father was Michael McCarthy and mother Sandra. She graduated from St. Francis Academy in Joliet, Illinois. Career

Melissa McCarthy’s first TV role was in ‘Jenny’ an NBC Comedy Series. The comedy film ‘Go’ (1999) was her first film which was a starting point of her long, illustrious career as an actor.

Her initial films “Drowning Mona’, ‘Disney’s The Kid’, ‘Charlie Angels’ and ‘The Life of David Gale’ recognised her as a foremost actor. She also did many TV series like ‘Kim Possible’ where she voiced DN Amy and was in the cast as Sookie St. James in Gilmore Girls.

In 2007 Melissa McCarthy starred in ‘The Nines’ a science fantasy opposite Ryan Reynolds. Her performance in the popular sitcom ‘Samantha Who?’ as Dena Stevens, a childhood friend of Samantha was widely received and appreciated.

In September 2010, Melissa McCarthy starred in CBS sitcom ‘Mike and Molly’. In 2011 she starred in a comedy film ‘Bridesmaid’ alongside Kristen Wiig and Maya Rudolph for which she was nominated for an Academy Award. This role of her was one of the best roles of her career if we go by several awards and nominations that she got for it.

Her Stellar performance continued with her outstanding role in a comedy series ‘Mike and Molly’ for which she won an Emmy Award for outstanding lead actress in a comedy series.

Melissa McCarthy was given recognition for her acting performances by invitation to join the Academy of motion pictures Arts and Science in June 2012. Her performance as a host in NBC ‘Saturday night live’ five times from 2011 to 2017 was one of her most celebrated performance, winning wide acclaim.

In 2013 her performance in the crime comedy ‘Identity Thief’ was a box office hit. It grossed $174 million worldwide. In late 2013 her performance in ‘The Heat’ was an even bigger success. It received critical acclaim and grossed $229 million worldwide.

Melissa McCarthy produced a CBS pilot in which her husband Ben Falcone starred. She co-wrote the script of the movie ‘Tammy’ in 2014.
